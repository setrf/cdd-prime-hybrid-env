# SUPER CDD Plan
Comprehensive, no-gap diligence execution plan covering commercial due diligence (CDD) plus integrated workstreams used in full buy-side diligence.

## 1) Scope and Rules
- Objective: De-risk investment decision, set bid guardrails, and define executable value creation plan.
- Standard timeline: 6-8 weeks pre-signing + confirmatory phase + Day 1/100-day readiness.
- Principle: Hypothesis-led, evidence-backed, cross-workstream synthesis from Day 1.
- Deliverables required:
  - Red Flag Memo (Week 1-2)
  - Interim Readouts (weekly)
  - IC Pack (commercial + integrated + valuation implications)
  - Bid Support Pack (price guardrails, SPA protections, negotiation points)
  - Day 1 and 100-Day Value Creation Plan

## 2) Phase Plan (Execution Backbone)

### Phase 0: Mobilize and Frame (Day 0-Day 3)
- Confirm investment thesis and top 10 value drivers.
- Translate thesis into testable hypotheses with pass/fail thresholds.
- Set up PMO, governance, cadence, and escalation protocol.
- Build integrated workplan across all streams.
- Launch data request list and management Q&A tracker.
- Freeze initial downside/upside scenario architecture.

### Phase 1: Rapid Fact Base and Red Flags (Week 1-2)
- Build initial market, customer, competition, and revenue quality fact base.
- Run first wave of expert and customer interviews.
- Identify top red flags and quantify first-order impact.
- Establish initial achievable plan view versus management plan.
- Issue Red Flag Memo and update bid posture if needed.

### Phase 2: Deep Dives and Triangulation (Week 2-4)
- Complete core CDD deep dives (market, customer, competition, pricing, GTM).
- Complete integrated streams (ops, tech, people, regulatory, tax/legal, ESG).
- Pressure-test business plan assumptions line by line.
- Quantify upside levers, downside risks, and execution constraints.
- Build synergy/dis-synergy model and cost-to-achieve.

### Phase 3: Synthesis and Bid Support (Week 4-6)
- Integrate all findings into single equity case and downside case.
- Finalize valuation implications and bid guardrails.
- Draft SPA protections and negotiation asks tied to findings.
- Align pre-close value creation thesis and 100-day initiatives.
- Prepare IC memo and decision pack.

### Phase 4: Confirmatory and Sign-to-Close (Week 6-Close)
- Revalidate critical assumptions with latest trading data.
- Close open data gaps and update sensitivities.
- Validate execution readiness for Day 1 and TSA dependencies.
- Convert diligence findings into signed execution charter.

### Phase 5: Day 1 to 100-Day Activation (Post-Close)
- Launch quick-win initiatives and KPI cadence.
- Resolve critical dependencies (systems, people, customer retention, pricing actions).
- Track initiative value realization versus underwrite.
- Prepare hold-period value proof points and exit-backcast milestones.

## 3) Master Workstream Checklist (All Streams)
Use this as the no-skip master list. Every workstream below should be explicitly marked `Done`, `In Progress`, `Blocked`, or `Not Applicable` with reason.

---

## WS01: Deal Thesis and Diligence Design
**Outputs:** Hypothesis tree, decision criteria, red-flag definitions, scenario framework.

- [ ] Define investment thesis, value-creation thesis, and deal-breakers.
- [ ] Convert thesis into testable hypotheses with evidence requirements.
- [ ] Identify IC-critical assumptions (growth, margin, capex, pricing, retention, concentration).
- [ ] Define pass/fail thresholds and escalation triggers per hypothesis.
- [ ] Build issue tree and link each branch to an owner/workstream.
- [ ] Prioritize critical questions for first 10 business days.
- [ ] Set base/upside/downside scenario logic and required proofs.
- [ ] Define explicit go/no-go decision rules.

## WS02: PMO, Governance, and Quality Control
**Outputs:** Integrated plan, RAID log, daily cadence, quality assurance protocol.

- [ ] Stand up integrated diligence PMO and governance model.
- [ ] Create master timeline and dependency map across all streams.
- [ ] Define daily standup and weekly steering cadence.
- [ ] Maintain RAID log (risks, assumptions, issues, dependencies).
- [ ] Maintain decision log and action register with owners/dates.
- [ ] Control document/model versioning.
- [ ] Set quality gates for analyses, models, and IC-ready outputs.
- [ ] Run cross-stream synthesis sessions to prevent silo conclusions.

## WS03: Data Room Intake, Validation, and Evidence Control
**Outputs:** Data quality report, reconciled KPI definitions, single source of truth files.

- [ ] Build detailed data request list by workstream and priority.
- [ ] Validate completeness, consistency, and recency of all key datasets.
- [ ] Reconcile KPI definitions between CIM, management deck, and extracts.
- [ ] Normalize one-offs, accounting changes, and calendar effects.
- [ ] Validate segment/geography/customer hierarchy mappings.
- [ ] Back-test management-reported KPIs against transaction-level data.
- [ ] Track data gaps and reopen requests with explicit deadlines.
- [ ] Lock audited data cuts for final model integration.

## WS04: Market Definition and Segmentation
**Outputs:** Market map, segment attractiveness matrix, adjacency map.

- [ ] Define product, customer, channel, and geographic market boundaries.
- [ ] Build market segmentation tree (use case, vertical, size, route-to-market).
- [ ] Distinguish TAM, SAM, SOM and served vs unserved opportunity.
- [ ] Size revenue and profit pools by segment.
- [ ] Map structural attractiveness by segment (growth, margin, intensity).
- [ ] Identify adjacency opportunities and white spaces.
- [ ] Validate where target has right-to-win vs weak position.

## WS05: Market Sizing and Growth Forecast
**Outputs:** Bottom-up and top-down market model, growth sensitivities.

- [ ] Build top-down market model from external sources.
- [ ] Build bottom-up model from demand drivers and unit economics.
- [ ] Triangulate with experts, channel checks, and customer inputs.
- [ ] Decompose historical growth into volume, price, and mix.
- [ ] Model forward growth by segment with macro/regulatory assumptions.
- [ ] Test cyclical resilience and recession performance.
- [ ] Build low/base/high market growth cases.
- [ ] Document confidence level for each growth driver.

## WS06: Customer and Voice-of-Customer (VoC)
**Outputs:** Customer truth set, retention/switching risk profile, NPS and need-gap insights.

- [ ] Define customer sampling plan (current, lost, prospects, channels).
- [ ] Conduct qualitative interviews and/or quantitative surveys.
- [ ] Measure purchase criteria and decision hierarchy by segment.
- [ ] Assess perceived differentiation vs alternatives.
- [ ] Identify switching barriers, triggers, and migration patterns.
- [ ] Analyze churn reasons and renewal/expansion dynamics.
- [ ] Assess account concentration and key-account fragility.
- [ ] Quantify wallet share and cross-sell headroom.

## WS07: Competitive Landscape and Position
**Outputs:** Competitor landscape, share dynamics, moat durability assessment.

- [ ] Build competitor map by segment/geography/price tier/channel.
- [ ] Benchmark product, service, and delivery performance.
- [ ] Analyze share trends and share-shift causes.
- [ ] Assess moat durability (IP, data, contracts, installed base, channel).
- [ ] Track competitor pricing behavior and promotional intensity.
- [ ] Evaluate substitute threats and disruption risk.
- [ ] Identify likely competitive responses post-transaction.

## WS08: Product, Portfolio, and Innovation
**Outputs:** Portfolio quality assessment, roadmap realism, innovation risk map.

- [ ] Analyze product/SKU profitability and margin mix.
- [ ] Evaluate product roadmap quality, timing, and dependencies.
- [ ] Assess new product pipeline conversion assumptions.
- [ ] Identify portfolio gaps and cannibalization risks.
- [ ] Evaluate feature parity by key segment.
- [ ] Review installed base dynamics and replacement cycles.
- [ ] Assess product governance and stage-gate rigor.
- [ ] Quantify portfolio optimization opportunities.

## WS09: Pricing and Monetization
**Outputs:** Pricing opportunity model, leakage map, implementation roadmap.

- [ ] Map list-to-net waterfall and discount leakage.
- [ ] Analyze realized price by segment/customer/channel/geography.
- [ ] Assess pricing architecture (good-better-best, pack/contract structures).
- [ ] Test willingness to pay and elasticity by segment/use case.
- [ ] Identify repricing pockets and contract reset windows.
- [ ] Analyze rebates/promotions/trade spend effectiveness.
- [ ] Evaluate pricing governance and exception control.
- [ ] Quantify upside, volume risk, and implementation complexity.

## WS10: Sales, Channel, and GTM Effectiveness
**Outputs:** GTM diagnosis, funnel improvement plan, channel risk map.

- [ ] Map full funnel performance by segment/channel/region.
- [ ] Analyze conversion bottlenecks and win/loss drivers.
- [ ] Benchmark sales productivity (quota attainment, ramp, coverage).
- [ ] Evaluate territory and account coverage design.
- [ ] Assess partner/channel economics and conflicts.
- [ ] Review sales compensation alignment with strategic priorities.
- [ ] Evaluate customer success and retention operating model.
- [ ] Quantify GTM uplift levers and required enablers.

## WS11: Marketing and Demand Generation
**Outputs:** ROMI analysis, demand model, priority growth plays.

- [ ] Audit marketing spend mix and ROMI by channel.
- [ ] Assess lead quality and sales handoff performance.
- [ ] Analyze CAC, payback, and cohort performance.
- [ ] Evaluate brand strength and differentiation by segment.
- [ ] Test messaging fit versus priority customer pain points.
- [ ] Identify underpenetrated segments and campaign opportunities.
- [ ] Quantify pipeline and revenue impact from marketing levers.

## WS12: Revenue Quality and Forecast Achievability
**Outputs:** Clean revenue bridge, forecast stress test, downside guardrails.

- [ ] Build revenue bridge (starting base + adds - churn +/- price/mix).
- [ ] Perform cohort analysis (retention, expansion, contraction, reactivation).
- [ ] Assess pipeline quality, aging, and conversion realism.
- [ ] Stress-test management forecast assumptions by segment.
- [ ] Model downside scenarios (demand shock, pricing pressure, churn rise).
- [ ] Identify dependence on one-offs or unsustainable commercial actions.
- [ ] Define confidence intervals for near- and medium-term forecast.

## WS13: Operations and Delivery Feasibility (Commercial Link)
**Outputs:** Operational feasibility report, bottleneck map, cost-to-serve baseline.

- [ ] Test capacity versus commercial plan by site/team/system.
- [ ] Identify bottlenecks in production, fulfillment, onboarding, or service delivery.
- [ ] Assess cost-to-serve by segment/product/channel.
- [ ] Evaluate footprint and network adequacy.
- [ ] Review procurement concentration and input-cost volatility exposure.
- [ ] Assess service-level performance and failure-mode risk.
- [ ] Validate implementation capacity for planned commercial initiatives.
- [ ] Quantify required operational investments and timing.

## WS14: Technology, Data, and Cyber Diligence
**Outputs:** Tech feasibility view, data readiness score, cyber risk profile.

- [ ] Assess architecture scalability against forecast volume and roadmap.
- [ ] Evaluate platform reliability, uptime, and incident patterns.
- [ ] Assess technical debt and refactor burden.
- [ ] Evaluate engineering org capacity and critical talent dependencies.
- [ ] Assess data architecture, governance, and analytics maturity.
- [ ] Review AI readiness and model/data constraints where relevant.
- [ ] Evaluate cybersecurity posture and material vulnerabilities.
- [ ] Estimate required remediation/modernization spend and timeline.

## WS15: People, Organization, and Culture
**Outputs:** Talent risk report, org effectiveness diagnosis, retention plan.

- [ ] Identify critical roles and key-person dependencies.
- [ ] Assess leadership quality, depth, and succession coverage.
- [ ] Analyze attrition patterns and retention risk hotspots.
- [ ] Evaluate organizational design (layers, spans, decision rights).
- [ ] Assess incentive plan alignment to growth and value targets.
- [ ] Evaluate capability gaps (sales, pricing, product, data, operations).
- [ ] Define Day 1 retention and capability build actions.
- [ ] Assess culture and change readiness for post-close execution.

## WS16: Regulatory, Antitrust, and Compliance (Commercial Impact)
**Outputs:** Regulatory risk register, approval timeline, mitigation plan.

- [ ] Identify required approvals and jurisdictional constraints.
- [ ] Assess antitrust/competition issues impacting structure or remedies.
- [ ] Review compliance obligations affecting product/channel/geography expansion.
- [ ] Analyze contract and policy constraints on repricing/commercial changes.
- [ ] Assess privacy/data rules affecting customer analytics and GTM.
- [ ] Quantify timing, cost, and execution impact of regulatory requirements.

## WS17: ESG and Sustainability (Commercial and Value Impact)
**Outputs:** ESG risk/opportunity map, ESG-linked value levers.

- [ ] Identify ESG factors with direct revenue or margin impact.
- [ ] Assess customer procurement requirements tied to ESG credentials.
- [ ] Evaluate operational sustainability risks in supply chain and footprint.
- [ ] Quantify ESG-related compliance and remediation spend.
- [ ] Identify ESG-driven growth/value levers (efficiency, access, premium positioning).
- [ ] Integrate ESG actions into 100-day and hold-period plan.

## WS18: Financial Due Diligence and QoE (Integrated Requirement)
**Outputs:** QoE bridge, normalized EBITDA/cash flow, net working capital view.

- [ ] Build QoE bridge from reported to normalized earnings.
- [ ] Analyze revenue recognition policies and consistency.
- [ ] Assess gross margin and operating expense normalization.
- [ ] Evaluate net working capital seasonality and true run-rate needs.
- [ ] Review capex intensity (maintenance vs growth).
- [ ] Validate debt-like items and contingent liabilities.
- [ ] Tie financial findings to commercial achievability assumptions.
- [ ] Reconcile integrated model assumptions with QoE outcomes.

## WS19: Tax Diligence (Integrated Requirement)
**Outputs:** Tax risk memo, structuring implications, post-close tax priorities.

- [ ] Assess historic tax compliance and uncertain tax positions.
- [ ] Review transfer pricing and cross-border tax risk.
- [ ] Evaluate indirect tax, payroll tax, and withholding exposures.
- [ ] Assess tax basis and transaction structuring options.
- [ ] Quantify tax leakage risks and remediation needs.
- [ ] Integrate tax implications into valuation and SPA protections.

## WS20: Legal and Contract Diligence (Integrated Requirement)
**Outputs:** Legal risk matrix, contract economics insights, SPA term proposals.

- [ ] Review top customer contracts for pricing/reset/termination rights.
- [ ] Review supplier/partner contracts for concentration and continuity risk.
- [ ] Assess IP ownership, licensing constraints, and infringement exposure.
- [ ] Identify litigation, claims, and contingent legal liabilities.
- [ ] Review employment agreements and change-of-control clauses.
- [ ] Assess data/privacy terms with commercial/tech impact.
- [ ] Translate legal findings into protections, covenants, and deal mechanics.

## WS21: Synergies and Dis-synergies
**Outputs:** Synergy model, dis-synergy risk model, cost-to-achieve plan.

- [ ] Build bottom-up cost synergy model by function and timing.
- [ ] Build revenue synergy model by segment/product/customer.
- [ ] Identify dis-synergies (churn, disruption, duplicate costs, TSA drag).
- [ ] Quantify cost-to-achieve and one-time implementation burden.
- [ ] Define initiative owners, milestones, and dependencies.
- [ ] Separate underwritten synergies from optional upside.
- [ ] Reconcile synergy assumptions to valuation and financing case.

## WS22: Value Creation Plan (Pre-close to Hold Period)
**Outputs:** Prioritized initiative backlog, KPI tree, value realization roadmap.

- [ ] Translate diligence findings into specific initiatives.
- [ ] Prioritize initiatives by value, speed, and implementation risk.
- [ ] Define 100-day quick wins versus 12-24 month transformations.
- [ ] Set KPI baselines, targets, owners, and review cadence.
- [ ] Define required resources, capex/opex, and enablement needs.
- [ ] Set dependency map and critical path.
- [ ] Build risk mitigation and fallback plans.
- [ ] Align sponsor and management on governance charter.

## WS23: Integration / Separation / TSA Readiness
**Outputs:** Day 1 blueprint, TSA plan, integration governance model.

- [ ] Define integration thesis tied directly to deal thesis.
- [ ] Set Day 1 minimum viable operating model.
- [ ] Map carve-out perimeter and TSA requirements (if relevant).
- [ ] Define TSA exit milestones and readiness criteria.
- [ ] Set clean-team boundaries and pre-close conduct rules.
- [ ] Build integration governance and IMO cadence.
- [ ] Plan customer/employee/partner communication sequencing.
- [ ] Define Day 1 critical decisions and accountabilities.

## WS24: Valuation Integration and IC/Bid Support
**Outputs:** Equity case, downside case, bid range, negotiation pack.

- [ ] Integrate all stream outputs into a single commercial-financial model.
- [ ] Build base/upside/downside valuation cases.
- [ ] Quantify value-at-risk for key uncertainty drivers.
- [ ] Set bid guardrails and walk-away triggers.
- [ ] Prepare negotiation asks tied to validated risks.
- [ ] Draft SPA mechanisms (indemnities, escrow, earnout, covenants).
- [ ] Prepare IC memo, exhibits, and management Q&A script.

## WS25: Confirmatory Diligence and Sign-to-Close Control
**Outputs:** Confirmatory memo, updated sensitivities, close-readiness status.

- [ ] Re-check critical assumptions with latest monthly/quarterly actuals.
- [ ] Validate no material deterioration in trading and pipeline health.
- [ ] Close outstanding data requests and unresolved diligence points.
- [ ] Confirm synergy assumptions with deeper operating detail.
- [ ] Update downside analysis and valuation guardrails.
- [ ] Finalize close conditions and risk mitigations.
- [ ] Confirm Day 1 readiness and owner accountability.

## WS26: Exit-Backcast (PE-Specific but Recommended)
**Outputs:** Exit-aligned KPI architecture, future buyer narrative readiness.

- [ ] Define expected exit archetypes and buyer diligence priorities.
- [ ] Build future equity story with required proof points.
- [ ] Set KPI instrumentation to evidence value creation claims.
- [ ] Sequence initiatives to maximize exit multiple and certainty.
- [ ] Create vendor-DD-ready data and documentation standards.
- [ ] Track narrative consistency between underwrite and actual delivery.

## WS27: Stakeholder and Communications Management
**Outputs:** Controlled communications plan, escalation playbook.

- [ ] Define communication protocol with sponsor, target, lenders, advisors.
- [ ] Prepare message tracks for employees, customers, and partners.
- [ ] Establish rumor/leak response and escalation protocol.
- [ ] Align internal and external narratives with verified diligence facts.
- [ ] Maintain decision-ready briefing notes for leadership.

## WS28: Clean Team, Confidentiality, and Information Controls
**Outputs:** Clean-team charter, access controls, compliance log.

- [ ] Define clean-team scope and protected data categories.
- [ ] Set role-based data access permissions and approvals.
- [ ] Implement restricted analysis workflow for sensitive data.
- [ ] Maintain confidentiality log and information wall compliance.
- [ ] Validate antitrust-safe sharing protocols throughout diligence.

## 4) Cross-Workstream Synthesis Tasks (Mandatory)
- [ ] Build single integrated assumptions register across all workstreams.
- [ ] Resolve assumption conflicts (example: commercial growth vs delivery capacity).
- [ ] Tie every major finding to explicit valuation impact.
- [ ] Tie every identified risk to mitigation and owner.
- [ ] Tie every upside lever to timeline and enabling conditions.
- [ ] Confirm no “orphan findings” left outside decision logic.
- [ ] Confirm every IC question has evidence-backed answer.

## 5) Core Weekly Cadence
- Daily: PMO standup (30 min) for blockers, data requests, and decision actions.
- Twice weekly: Cross-stream synthesis session.
- Weekly: Steering committee review and decision updates.
- Weekly: Draft-to-final issue escalation and re-prioritization.
- End of Week 2: Red Flag Memo.
- End of Week 4-5: Full integration draft.
- End of Week 6: IC pack and bid support final.

## 6) Required Final Outputs (No-Gap Pack)
- [ ] Red Flag Memo with quantified impacts.
- [ ] Full CDD report (market, customer, competition, product, pricing, GTM, forecast).
- [ ] Integrated diligence appendix (ops, tech/cyber, people, legal, tax, ESG, regulatory).
- [ ] Synergy/dis-synergy and cost-to-achieve model.
- [ ] Consolidated base/upside/downside valuation cases.
- [ ] Negotiation and SPA protections memo.
- [ ] Day 1 readiness plan.
- [ ] 100-day value creation plan with KPI governance.
- [ ] Exit-backcast scorecard.

- [ ] Thesis and hypothesis tree fully tested.
- [ ] Market growth and segmentation triangulated.
- [ ] Customer truth validated across current/lost/prospective customers.
- [ ] Competitive position stress-tested with external evidence.
- [ ] Pricing upside and risk fully quantified.
- [ ] GTM effectiveness and operating feasibility reconciled.
- [ ] Forecast achievability pressure-tested by scenario.
- [ ] Technology/data/cyber constraints linked to commercial plan.
- [ ] Talent/org risks linked to execution plan.
- [ ] Regulatory/legal/tax/ESG risks quantified and mitigated.
- [ ] Synergy plan reconciled with integration capacity.
- [ ] IC/bid recommendations fully linked to evidence.
- [ ] Day 1 and 100-day plan owner-ready and funded.
